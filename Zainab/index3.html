My Journey With Books

There are so many, too many perhaps, things that my mom has taught me throughout my childhood that make me eternally grateful to her. It’s really a lifelong list of lessons and on top of this list is how she introduced me to books since I was a few months old.


She would tell me stories from her big imagination, which I proudly inherited, and I would refuse to sleep without one. This, telling me or reading a story every night, had a major effect on both my speaking skills and writing skills, which I developed before I could even write. I would make up a story and my mom would write it down. Then, of course, I got older and started writing for myself.


My mom LOVED the stories I wrote. But, when I got to 5th grade I had found different hobbies and didn’t enjoy writing as much as I used to, so I stopped. This upset my mom so she constantly reminded me to write. At that age, I would do the opposite of whatever someone would tell me. Yup, I stopped for good and, I hated reading books


First year of middle school, I got back to reading and I still remember how I spent three days reading a book and feeling absolutely lost in it. This reminded me of why I loved reading in the first place. It was my escape from reality. Since then, I have got back to reading, but unfortunately I couldn’t get back to writing. I still have the imagination to come up with great stories, but I lost my writing skills.


Eventually, I developed a beautiful relationship with books. I loved/ love all kinds of books but there’re definitely some genres I enjoy more than others. No matter where I’m going, I will always have a book with me.
